# House Hacking Strategies

## FHA Loan Requirements for House Hacking

### Down Payment
- **3.5% down** with 580+ credit score
- **10% down** with 500-579 credit score
- Must be owner-occupied (primary residence for at least 12 months)
- 1-4 unit properties eligible

### Owner-Occupancy Rules
- Must move in within 60 days of closing
- Must occupy as primary residence for at least 1 year
- After 1 year, may convert to investment property and purchase a new primary residence with FHA
- Cannot have more than one FHA loan at a time (exceptions for relocation, growing family)

### FHA Property Requirements
- Must meet HUD minimum property standards
- Appraisal required (not just inspection)
- Self-sufficiency test for 3-4 unit properties: rental income from non-owner units must cover mortgage payment

### FHA Self-Sufficiency Test (3-4 Units)
- Net rental income (gross rent x 75%) from all units must exceed the mortgage payment
- Only applies to 3-4 unit properties, not duplexes
- Uses appraiser's market rent estimate, not actual lease amounts

## Multi-Unit Analysis

### Duplex House Hack
- **Pros**: Simplest entry, one tenant to manage, strong loan options
- **Cons**: Only one rental unit, lower offset potential
- **Target offset**: 50-70% of mortgage covered
- **Best for**: First-time investors, low management tolerance

### Triplex House Hack
- **Pros**: Two rental units, higher offset potential, still FHA-eligible
- **Cons**: More management, self-sufficiency test applies
- **Target offset**: 70-100% of mortgage covered
- **Best for**: Investors wanting near-zero housing cost

### Quadplex House Hack
- **Pros**: Maximum units under residential financing, highest offset
- **Cons**: Most management intensive, self-sufficiency test applies, harder to find
- **Target offset**: 100-130% of mortgage (positive cash flow while living there)
- **Best for**: Serious investors, those comfortable with property management

## ADU (Accessory Dwelling Unit) Strategy

### Types
- **Detached ADU**: Separate structure on the lot (garage conversion, new build)
- **Attached ADU**: Addition to main house
- **Internal ADU**: Basement or attic conversion
- **Junior ADU**: Conversion within existing footprint (California-specific)

### Considerations
- Check local zoning and permitting requirements
- Construction costs: $50,000-$200,000+ depending on type and market
- Potential to add $500-$2,000+/month rental income
- May increase property value by 20-30% of construction cost
- Permitting timeline: 2-6 months typical

## Room Rental (Rent-by-the-Room) Strategy

### Approach
- Purchase single-family home, rent individual rooms
- Owner occupies one bedroom
- Each room rented separately at a premium vs per-unit rent

### Considerations
- Gross rent typically 30-50% higher than renting the entire unit
- Higher management burden (shared spaces, roommate dynamics)
- Furnishing costs: $2,000-$5,000 per room
- Check local regulations on number of unrelated occupants
- Short-term rental potential (Airbnb) for individual rooms

### Screening Criteria
- Background and credit checks for each tenant
- Lease should specify shared space rules, quiet hours, guest policies
- Common area maintenance responsibilities

## House Hack Scoring Criteria

### Mortgage Offset Score (0-100)

| Offset % | Score | Label |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| 100%+ | 90-100 | Full offset + cash flow |
| 80-99% | 70-89 | Near-full offset |
| 60-79% | 50-69 | Strong offset |
| 40-59% | 30-49 | Moderate offset |
| 20-39% | 10-29 | Minimal offset |
| < 20% | 0-9 | Negligible offset |

### Evaluation Checklist
1. **Financing**: FHA eligible? Down payment amount? Monthly PITI?
2. **Rental Income**: Market rent for non-owner units? Room rental potential?
3. **Offset Calculation**: Rental income / Total PITI
4. **Exit Strategy**: What happens after year 1? Rent all units? Sell? Refi?
5. **Livability**: Is the owner unit comfortable? Adequate privacy?
6. **Upside**: Value-add potential? ADU opportunity? Rent growth projection?

### Post-Year-1 Exit Strategies
- **Hold and rent all units**: Convert to full investment property, move out
- **Refinance**: Remove FHA MIP via conventional refi at 80% LTV
- **BRRRR transition**: Renovate, refi, repeat with next house hack
- **Sell**: Capture appreciation, use profits for next investment
- **Continue living**: Enjoy subsidized housing, save aggressively
