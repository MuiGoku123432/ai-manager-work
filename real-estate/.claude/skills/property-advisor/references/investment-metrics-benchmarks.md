# Investment Metrics and Benchmarks

## Cap Rate (Capitalization Rate)

### Formula
Cap Rate = Net Operating Income (NOI) / Property Value x 100

### NOI Calculation
NOI = Gross Rental Income - Vacancy Loss - Operating Expenses

Operating expenses include: property taxes, insurance, maintenance, property management, HOA, utilities (if owner-paid). Exclude: mortgage payments, capital expenditures, depreciation.

### Benchmarks by Market Type
| Market Type | Cap Rate Range | Notes |
|-------------|---------------|-------|
| Class A (Urban Core) | 3-5% | Lower risk, lower return, appreciation play |
| Class B (Suburban) | 5-7% | Balanced risk/return, good for house hacking |
| Class C (Workforce) | 7-10% | Higher cash flow, more management intensive |
| Class D (Distressed) | 10%+ | High risk, highest cash flow potential |

### Cap Rate Red Flags
- Below 3%: Likely negative cash flow with financing
- Above 12%: Investigate why — may indicate high risk area or deferred maintenance

## Cash-on-Cash Return (CoC)

### Formula
CoC = Annual Pre-Tax Cash Flow / Total Cash Invested x 100

### Cash Invested Includes
- Down payment
- Closing costs
- Rehab/renovation costs
- Reserves (if set aside at purchase)

### Benchmarks
| CoC Return | Assessment |
|------------|-----------|
| < 4% | Below savings account, reconsider |
| 4-8% | Acceptable if strong appreciation market |
| 8-12% | Good — standard target |
| 12-15% | Very good — strong deal |
| 15%+ | Excellent — verify assumptions carefully |

---

## Internal Rate of Return (IRR)

### Components
- Annual cash flow
- Mortgage principal paydown
- Property appreciation
- Tax benefits
- Sale proceeds (net of selling costs)

### Benchmarks
| Time Horizon | Minimum IRR | Target IRR |
|-------------|-------------|------------|
| 5 years | 12% | 15-20% |
| 10 years | 10% | 12-18% |
| 20+ years | 8% | 10-15% |

## Rent-to-Price Ratios

### The 1% Rule
- Monthly rent should be at least 1% of purchase price
- Example: $200,000 property should rent for $2,000+/month
- Quick screening tool — not a final decision metric
- Increasingly difficult to achieve in appreciation markets

### The 2% Rule
- Monthly rent at 2%+ of purchase price
- Extremely rare in most markets
- When found, usually indicates Class C/D property with higher risk
- Common only in very low-cost markets

### Practical Guidance
| Ratio | Assessment |
|-------|-----------|
| < 0.5% | Pure appreciation play, negative cash flow likely |
| 0.5-0.7% | Appreciation market, marginal cash flow |
| 0.7-1.0% | Balanced market, achievable cash flow with good terms |
| 1.0-1.5% | Strong cash flow market |
| 1.5%+ | Exceptional cash flow, verify market fundamentals |

## DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio)

### Formula
DSCR = Net Operating Income / Annual Debt Service

### Benchmarks
| DSCR | Assessment |
|------|-----------|
| < 1.0 | Negative cash flow — income doesn't cover debt |
| 1.0-1.15 | Break-even, no margin for error |
| 1.15-1.25 | Minimum for most DSCR lenders |
| 1.25-1.50 | Healthy coverage, standard target |
| 1.50+ | Strong coverage, conservative position |

### DSCR Loan Qualification
- Most DSCR lenders require minimum 1.15-1.25
- No personal income verification required
- Higher DSCR = better rate and terms
- Typically require 20-25% down payment

## Gross Rent Multiplier (GRM)

### Formula
GRM = Property Price / Annual Gross Rent

### Benchmarks
| GRM | Assessment |
|-----|-----------|
| < 8 | Strong cash flow potential |
| 8-12 | Good balance of cash flow and appreciation |
| 12-15 | Appreciation-weighted market |
| 15-20 | Heavy appreciation play |
| 20+ | Speculative — cash flow unlikely |

## Expense Ratios by Property Type

### Operating Expense as % of Gross Rent
| Property Type | Expense Ratio | Notes |
|---------------|--------------|-------|
| Single-family | 35-45% | Lower if tenant pays utilities |
| Duplex | 40-50% | Shared systems may reduce costs |
| Triplex/Quad | 45-55% | More units = more maintenance |
| 5+ units (commercial) | 45-60% | Professional management required |

### Standard Expense Assumptions (Single-Family)
| Expense | % of Gross Rent | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|-------|
| Vacancy | 5-8% | Market dependent |
| Property Management | 8-10% | Even if self-managing, budget this |
| Maintenance/Repairs | 8-10% | Higher for older properties |
| Capital Expenditures | 5-8% | Roof, HVAC, appliances reserves |
| Property Taxes | Varies | Use actual tax amount |
| Insurance | 3-5% | Landlord policy, may need flood/wind |
| HOA | Varies | If applicable |

### The 50% Rule (Quick Estimate)
- Assume 50% of gross rent goes to operating expenses (excluding mortgage)
- NOI estimate = Gross Rent x 50%
- Useful for quick screening, refine with actual expenses for analysis

## BRRRR Strategy Metrics

### Key Metrics
- **All-in cost**: Purchase price + rehab + closing + holding costs
- **After Repair Value (ARV)**: Appraised value post-renovation
- **Cash-out refi**: Typically 70-75% of ARV
- **Cash left in deal**: All-in cost - refi proceeds
- **Infinite return**: Achieved when refi covers 100% of cash invested

### BRRRR Benchmarks
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Purchase at % of ARV | 65-75% |
| Rehab budget accuracy | Within 10% of estimate |
| Cash left in deal | $0-$10,000 (ideally none) |
| Post-refi CoC | 15%+ (infinite if no cash left) |
| Rehab timeline | 2-4 months |

## Fix-and-Flip Metrics

### Key Metrics
- **ARV**: After repair value
- **MAO (Maximum Allowable Offer)**: ARV x 70% - Rehab costs
- **Profit margin**: Net profit / ARV
- **ROI**: Net profit / Total cash invested
- **Holding costs**: Months x (loan interest + taxes + insurance + utilities)

### Flip Benchmarks
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Minimum net profit | $25,000+ |
| Profit margin | 10-15% of ARV |
| ROI | 20%+ per flip |
| 70% Rule compliance | MAO at or below 70% ARV - rehab |
| Timeline | 3-6 months total (buy to sell) |
